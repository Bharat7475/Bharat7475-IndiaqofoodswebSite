Storage

Preservation of Freshness

Proper storage techniques involve maintaining optimal conditions such as temperature, humidity, and airflow to slow down the natural degradation processes of food. For example, refrigeration or controlled atmosphere storage can significantly extend the shelf life of fresh produce by inhibiting the growth of spoilage microorganisms and enzymes.

Prevention of Contamination

Packaging materials must be carefully selected to provide an effective barrier against contaminants while also ensuring that they do not impart any harmful substances to the food. This involves considerations such as selecting non-toxic materials, preventing migration of chemicals from packaging into food, contamination through proper sealing and hygiene practices during packaging.

Transportation and Handling

Packaging must withstand the rigors of transportation and handling throughout the supply chain, including loading, unloading, stacking, and transit. Robust packaging materials and designs, along with proper labeling and stacking instructions, help minimize the risk of damage or contamination during transit.
